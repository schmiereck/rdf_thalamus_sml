## Root Cause Analysis

### 1. Can ANY local training objective reach ≥60% accuracy?
**No.** The best diagnostic result is ~43.4% (random embedding only). All trained configurations perform **worse** than this.

### 2. Is the hierarchy itself the problem?
**No.** The untrained hierarchical baseline achieves **~51.1%** accuracy. The architecture itself is capable of representing class structure. The problem is exclusively in the training objective.

### 3. Does simultaneous training help over progressive training?
**Marginally.** Simultaneous training (41.5%) slightly outperforms single-layer (39.8%) and strong L1 (37.1%), but it is still far below the untrained baseline. Inter-layer coordination is not the bottleneck.

### 4. Does lateral inhibition / predictive coding help?
**No.** Despite achieving very high sparsity (~72%), Exp4 is the *worst* on average (38.2%) and highly unstable (σ=11.9%, ranging from 20% to 53% across seeds). The competition mechanism creates sparse codes but destroys the discriminative structure needed for classification.

### 5. Can stronger L1 create meaningful sparse codes?
**No.** Even with 25× stronger L1 (0.05 vs 0.002), sparsity remains near 0% (0.4%) and accuracy drops to ~37%. The `Normal(0,1)` embeddings prevent the previous collapse issue, but the L1 penalty is still too weak to induce sparsity in tanh activations.

## Critical Finding

**The reconstruction objective (MSE + L1) is actively harmful.** Every trained variant performs worse than the untrained baseline, including:
- Single-layer (fewer parameters, simpler)
- Full hierarchy with simultaneous training
- Full hierarchy with lateral inhibition
- Full hierarchy with strong sparsity penalty

This means the autoencoder is learning to reconstruct *local pixel patterns* that are shared across categories (e.g., edges, blobs), rather than learning *category-discriminative features*. The training objective optimizes for input reconstruction fidelity, which is fundamentally misaligned with the classification goal.

## Recommended Next Step

Since no local objective works, the architecture likely needs a **task-aligned supervisory signal** at some level of the hierarchy—either:
1. A classification-aware objective (e.g., supervised contrastive, or a class-conditional reconstruction)
2. Direct end-to-end fine-tuning with cross-entropy
3. A hybrid approach where lower layers use local objectives and upper layers receive class supervision
