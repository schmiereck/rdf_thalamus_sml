## Phase 5 Experiment Complete — Key Findings

The full 20-experiment suite (30 epochs × 5 seeds × 4 variants) finished successfully (~30 minutes total). Here are the key findings from `phase_5/REPORT.md`:

---

### 1. Classification Accuracy

| Variant | Mean Test Acc | Std |
|--------|-------------:|:---:|
| **P5-A** | **0.8300** | 0.0227 |
| P5-C | 0.8255 | 0.0231 |
| P5-B | 0.8195 | 0.0250 |
| untrained | 0.5210 | 0.0356 |

All three training variants achieve ~82–83% accuracy, dramatically above the untrained baseline (~52%). P5-A (pure emergence) performed best, though differences are marginal. **P5-B passed F4** (82.0% > 75% threshold, target 78%).

---

### 2. Semantic Consistency Scores

| Variant | Overall | Within-Layer | Cross-Layer |
|--------|--------:|-------------:|------------:|
| P5-A | **0.714** | **0.974** | 0.580 |
| P5-B | 0.726 | 0.973 | 0.534 |
| P5-C | 0.712 | 0.976 | 0.545 |
| untrained | 0.675 | 0.944 | 0.447 |

- **Within-layer consistency is extremely high** (~0.97) across all variants — code dimensions consistently map to the same semantic across spatial positions.
- **Cross-layer consistency is moderate** (~0.54–0.58), suggesting semantic roles shift across layers.
- Training provides a modest but meaningful **absolute +0.039 training gain** over untrained (0.714 vs 0.675).

---

### 3. P5-B Anchored vs Free Dimension Consistency

- **Anchored dims (0–4):** 0.700
- **Free dims (5–15):** 0.738
- **P5-A for comparison:** 0.714

Surprisingly, free dimensions are slightly more consistent than anchored ones, and the anchored dimensions are not significantly better than P5-A's overall. The anchor improvement is -0.014.

---

### 4. Falsification Criteria Results

| Criterion | Description | Status | Detail |
|-----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| **F1** | Overall consistency ≤ 0.30 | ✅ **PASS** | P5-A = 0.714 |

| **F4** | P5-B accuracy < 75% | ✅ **PASS** | P5-B = 82.0% |
| **F5** | P5-B anchor improvement < 0.05 | ⚠️ **TRIGGERED** | Improvement = -0.014 |

---

### Verdict: **PARTIALLY REFUTED**

The primary hypothesis that "code dimensions carry consistent, interpretable semantics" receives **partial support**:

- ✅ Strong support: Very high within-layer consistency (0.974), far above chance (0.20)
- ✅ Strong support: Training substantially improves classification (83% vs 52%)
- ⚠️ Challenge: Training gain in consistency is modest (+0.039), just under the 0.05 threshold
- ⚠️ Challenge: Anchor regularization in P5-B did not improve consistency — anchored dims (0.700) were actually slightly *less* consistent than free dims (0.738) or P5-A (0.714)

### R² Pattern Insight

The R-squared heatmaps reveal that:
- **Magnitude** dominates many dimensions (R² up to 0.85 in temporal layers)
- **Gradient** captures a distinct semantic axis in several dims (R² up to 0.61)
- **Variance** and **novelty** are present but weaker signals
- **Periodicity** is nearly absent across all variants (R² < 0.06)
